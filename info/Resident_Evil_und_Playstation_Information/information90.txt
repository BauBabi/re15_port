12 - BLK (Block/Unknown)

It is currently unknown what BLK, Block data really does. The data is not required to be in a room, there are some rooms that don't have it at all and yet they work without issues.

When representing them in gamespace, they look like inner boundaries and they might be a left over when the developers designed the rooms. This is a theory though, it's hard to say at this moment.

But we know at least how the data is structured and also how it is represented.
The first thing that comes is a 4 byte long value, which you could call a small header.

BLK Header Structure (4 Bytes)
00&#124; Amount
Amount (4 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Obviously defining the amount of BLK entries that will follow now.
Followed by the "header" which as you can see, only give us the amount of objects to come, an array of 12 byte long BLK entries will start.

BLK Entry Structure (12 Bytes)
00&#124; X1
02&#124; Z1
04&#124; X2
06&#124; Z2
08&#124; Dir_bit
10&#124; Abut
X1 (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The first X coordinate, from which the game will start drawing a rectangle shaped "block".

Z1 (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The first Z coordinate, from which the game will start drawing a rectangle shaped "block".

X2 (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The second X coordinate, defining to which point the "block" will be drawn.

Z2  (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
The second Z coordinate, defining to which point the "block" will be drawn.

Dir_bit (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
It's currently unknown what this value's role is. Dir, in it's name could mean it has some role in setting something direction related?!?...

Dir_bit (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
It's currently unknown what this value's role is. Abut, could mean "attribute", but what kind of attribute is it?
>>> The data is then followed by the 11 - FLR section.
Note:
BLK data seems to be "optional, it does not have to be in a room, for the room to work. Since of it still unsolved role it is hard to say, what is effected by it after all.
