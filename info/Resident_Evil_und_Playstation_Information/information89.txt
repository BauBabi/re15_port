11 - FLR (Floor/Footstep sound areas)

FLR data handles areas that will define in which section of the room, which footstep sound will be used.
The data is not required to be in a room to have a working RDT, however missing FLR data will lead to no footstep sounds being used in the room.

In terms of how to represent them, it's very similar to the way boundaries are "drawn", FLR data also is a 2D plane placed on the floor of the room.

Similar to the 12 - BLK data, the FLR data section will also start with
a small "header" which again just offers the amount of FLR entries being in place.

FLR Header Structure (2 Bytes)
00&#124; Amount
Amount (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Obviously defining the amount of FLR entries that will follow now.
Followed by the "header" which as you can see, only give us the amount of objects to come, an array of 12 byte long FLR entries will start.

FLR Entry Structure (12 Bytes)
00&#124; X
02&#124; Z
04&#124; W
06&#124; D
08&#124; Sound ID
10&#124; Dummy
X (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Defining the X value from where the floor area is "drawn".

Z (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Defining the Z value from where the floor area is "drawn".

W (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Defining the width of the floor area, starting from the X coordinate.

D  (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
Defining the density of the floor area, starting from the Z coordinate.

Sound ID (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
It refers to a .SAP soundfile that contains the footstep sounds to use. If it's value was 26 it would mean it uses FS26.SAP in the game's
"...&#092;COMMON&#092;SOUND&#092;ROOM&#092;" folder.

Dummy (2 Bytes)
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------
It's usually just 0 and therefore is likely a dummy value.
>>> The data is then followed by the 16 - SCD Main section.
Note:
FLR data seems to be "optional", it does not have to be in a room. Meaning missing FLR data won't lead to the room causing a crash. However, you would then have
a room that has no footstep sounds in use.
